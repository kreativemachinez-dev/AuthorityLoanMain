E-CONSENT DISCLOSURE

At Digital Consulting Group LLC, we provide electronic disclosures, records, and signatures to facilitate transactions between consumers and lenders. By using our platform, you agree to receive electronic communications in place of paper documents. This disclosure outlines your rights and obligations regarding electronic transactions and e-consent.

1. Consent to Electronic Transactions

By submitting your information, you expressly consent to:

  • Receive electronic disclosures, notices, and communications related to loan inquiries, approvals, and transactions.
  • Sign agreements electronically in compliance with the E-SIGN Act.
  • Allow lenders and financial service providers to provide required documents digitally.

Acknowledgment Requirement: Before proceeding with any transaction, you must actively check a box or provide digital acknowledgment confirming that you have read and agree to this E-Consent Disclosure.

Third-Party Lenders’ E-Consent: Some lenders may require separate e-consent agreements. Digital Consulting Group LLC is not responsible for a lender’s e-signature policies, and users must review each lender’s terms before proceeding.

2. Right to Receive Paper Copies

You may request a paper copy of any electronically provided document by contacting us at:

authorityloan.com

Denver, Colorado 80202
Email: support@authorityloan.com

Requests for paper copies will not revoke your prior e-consent, and a reasonable fee may apply.

3. Right to Withdraw Consent

You have the right to withdraw your e-consent at any time by notifying us in writing. However, withdrawing consent may:

  • Prevent you from completing transactions with participating lenders electronically.
  • Require you to submit loan applications and receive disclosures in paper form.

Processing time for withdrawal requests is up to 10 business days.

4. Updating Your Contact Information

To continue receiving electronic communications, it is your responsibility to keep your email address and phone number updated. If your contact information changes, notify us immediately using the details provided above.

5. Hardware and Software Requirements

To access and retain electronic records, you must have:

  • A device with internet access (computer, tablet, smartphone).
  • A web browser that supports secure encryption.
  • A valid email account to receive communications.
  • Software to open PDF documents (e.g., Adobe Acrobat Reader).
  • A printer or storage device if you choose to retain copies.

If technology requirements change, we will notify you and provide an opportunity to withdraw consent.

6. Governing Law & Acceptance

This E-Consent Disclosure is governed by the E-SIGN Act and applicable federal and state laws. By submitting your information and checking the acknowledgment box, you confirm that you:

  • Have read and understand this E-Consent Disclosure.
  • Agree to conduct transactions electronically with Digital Consulting Group LLC and third-party lenders.
  • Acknowledge that lenders may have additional e-consent requirements.

If you do not agree to these terms, do not proceed with submitting your information on this website.

For questions or assistance, contact us using the information above.

Consumer Risk Disclosure

Borrowing money through short-term and installment loans carries financial risks. High-interest loans may not be suitable for all borrowers, and failure to repay may result in additional fees, collection efforts, or negative impacts on your credit score. Before accepting any loan offer, carefully review the lender’s terms, repayment schedule, and total loan cost to ensure it aligns with your financial situation. If you are facing financial hardship, consider exploring alternative lending options or consulting a financial advisor.

APR Variation Notice

The Annual Percentage Rate (APR) for loans varies depending on lender policies, state regulations, creditworthiness, and loan terms. APRs typically range from 5.99% to 35.99%, but actual APRs may be higher or lower depending on lender policies, loan duration, borrower creditworthiness, state regulations, and additional factors. Lenders are required by law to disclose the exact APR, all fees, repayment schedules, and total loan cost before you agree to the loan.
